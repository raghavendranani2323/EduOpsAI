# Customer Needs, ICP, And Positioning

## Segment Analysis

| Segment | Must-Haves | Adoption Blockers | Retention Drivers | WTP Outlook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Small private schools | Student records, attendance, fees, notices, exams/report cards, parent communication, academic-year rollover. | Need report cards, transport/library expectations, principal/admin buy-in, migration anxiety. | Reliable fee collection, parent trust, academic records, support. | Moderate, but feature breadth and support burden are high. |
| Medium private schools | Full ERP, approvals, transport, payroll, inventory, custom roles, reporting, integrations. | Long sales cycle, customisation, security review, data migration. | Deep workflow lock-in and reports. | Higher, but not ideal first ICP. |
| Coaching centres | Student batches, attendance, fees, admissions follow-up, WhatsApp reminders, tests/marks, parent updates. | Existing Classplus/WhatsApp/spreadsheets, price sensitivity, owner time. | Faster fee recovery, fewer missed follow-ups, batch visibility. | Strongest near-term fit. |
| Preschools | Parent communication, photos/activity updates, attendance, fee reminders, child safety trust, consent. | Trust, media workflows, parent expectations, privacy. | Parent engagement and daily updates. | Good later if communication/media/consent mature. |
| Independent tuition centres | Simple student list, attendance, monthly fee due, WhatsApp reminders. | Very price sensitive; may resist setup. | Owner saves time and collects faster. | Low per centre but good for product simplicity. |

## Stakeholder Jobs To Be Done

| Stakeholder | Daily Job | EduOps Must Prove |
|---|---|---|
| Owner | Know fees due, admissions pipeline, attendance status, and staff activity. | Accurate dashboard, daily collections, follow-ups, no data loss. |
| Principal/Admin | Maintain students/classes/staff, notices, exams, records. | Simple setup, reliable permissions, audit trail, support. |
| Accountant | Record collections, issue receipts, track dues, reconcile cash/UPI/online. | Financial correctness, reports, receipt integrity, duplicate protection. |
| Teacher | Mark attendance, assign homework, enter marks, see assigned classes only. | Fast mobile workflows and strict role scope. |
| Admissions counsellor | Follow up leads and convert students. | Call/WhatsApp action, follow-up reminders, duplicate detection. |
| Parent | See fees/notices/homework/attendance and pay or contact school. | Trustworthy mobile portal, language clarity, payment confidence. |

## Recommended ICP

Owner-led coaching and tuition centres in Tier 2/Tier 3 Indian cities:

- 100-800 students.
- 3-25 staff.
- Monthly/term fee collection.
- Parents already use WhatsApp.
- Owner or accountant maintains spreadsheets/registers.
- No full ERP requirement.
- Wants better fee visibility and fewer missed follow-ups.

Why this ICP:

- Daily pain is acute and repeated.
- Decision maker is close to daily operations.
- Sales cycle is shorter than schools.
- Product breadth required is achievable.
- Mobile-first and WhatsApp-first design matters.
- EduOps can avoid competing directly on transport/library/payroll/report-card breadth.

## Positioning

Primary:

"Mobile-first fee, attendance, and parent communication software for Indian coaching and tuition centres."

Supporting copy:

- Replace fee registers and scattered spreadsheets.
- Mark attendance quickly from a phone.
- Know who has not paid this month.
- Send WhatsApp fee reminders and notices.
- Track admissions follow-ups.
- Give parents one simple mobile link.

Avoid:

- "Complete school ERP" until breadth is real.
- "AI-powered" unless a specific AI workflow exists and is useful.
- "Offline-first" until service worker/sync/conflict handling are production-ready.

## Willingness-To-Pay Factors

Evidence-backed assumptions requiring customer interviews:

- Owners pay when fee recovery improves or admin time drops visibly.
- Payment willingness is strongest when the product helps collect overdue fees faster.
- WhatsApp-first reminders are more valuable than email/SMS for many small institutions.
- Transparent pricing matters because competitors often use opaque/contact-sales pricing.
- Founder-led migration/support can overcome setup fear.

Possible pricing hypothesis:

- Starter: Rs 999-1,499/month for up to 200 students.
- Growth: Rs 1,999-2,999/month for up to 600 students.
- Pro: Rs 4,999/month for up to 1,500 students, advanced reports, more staff, provider-integrated messaging.
- Annual discount: 15-20 percent.
- One-time assisted setup/import: Rs 3,000-10,000 depending data quality.

This requires validation through 10-20 buyer interviews and 3-5 pilots.

## Adoption Blockers

- Fear of losing data.
- Staff not comfortable with software.
- Poor internet and shared devices.
- Existing WhatsApp habits.
- Price sensitivity.
- Migration from old registers/spreadsheets.
- Concern over online payment reconciliation.
- Parent privacy concerns.
- No visible support/phone/WhatsApp contact.

## Retention Drivers

- Daily fee due list.
- Faster payment follow-up.
- Reliable attendance history.
- Parent trust in receipts and notices.
- Admissions follow-up reminders.
- Owner dashboard showing cash/UPI/online collections.
- Easy data export.
- Responsive support during exam/admission/fee cycles.

## Activation Metrics

- First class created.
- 50+ students imported or created.
- First staff member invited.
- First attendance saved.
- First fee plan created.
- First invoice batch generated.
- First payment recorded.
- First WhatsApp reminder shared.
- First notice/homework published.
- First parent portal link opened.

## Retention Metrics

- Weekly active owner/admin.
- Teacher attendance saves per class per week.
- Monthly invoices generated.
- Payment recording completion rate.
- Overdue follow-up actions.
- Parent portal opens.
- Homework/notice publishes.
- Admissions follow-ups completed on time.
