## Positive Patterns To Learn From

- Competitors sell outcomes, not database modules: fee collection, parent communication, attendance, admissions, reports.
- Mature products show support, onboarding, and implementation assistance prominently.
- School ERP buyers expect many modules, but small institutions often pay for the few daily workflows they actually use.
- App-store presence matters for parent/teacher trust, even if a mobile PWA is technically enough.
- Pricing is often opaque. Transparent starter pricing can differentiate for small centres.

## Common Complaint Themes And Opportunities

Supported by review-marketplace snippets and competitor positioning:

- Over-complex setup and learning curve.
- Sales-led or opaque pricing.
- Heavy ERP breadth that small institutions do not need.
- Support/customisation dependency.
- Mobile app quality and performance can vary.
- Lock-in and data portability concerns.
- Small institutions are often forced to pay for modules they do not use.

EduOps opportunity:

- Be "small-institution operational software", not "another full ERP".
- Offer guided setup in one day.
- Make export/data ownership obvious.
- Make fee collection, attendance, and WhatsApp reminders excellent before adding transport/library/payroll.
- Use transparent pricing and founder-led onboarding.

## Features Not Worth Copying Now

- 100+ module ERP breadth.
- Transport/library/inventory/hostel before first ICP validation.
- Heavy customisation engines.
- Native branded apps for every institution before payment/retention is proven.
- Generic AI summaries without daily operational value.

## Differentiation Hypothesis

EduOps can differentiate if it becomes:

1. Faster to set up than broad school ERPs.
2. Easier to use on low-cost Android phones.
3. Stronger for fee follow-up and WhatsApp-first operations.
4. Transparent about pricing, data export, privacy, and support.
5. Safer and more reliable than low-cost legacy vendors.

This differentiation is not yet defensible until payment, offline, communication, security, and support gaps are fixed.
